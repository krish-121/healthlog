Understanding Your Heart

Knowledge is the first step to better heart health. Learn about arrhythmia, common triggers, and evidence-based strategies.

What is Arrhythmia?

Overview

Arrhythmia is an irregular heartbeat — your heart may beat too fast (tachycardia), too slow (bradycardia), or with an irregular rhythm. While occasional flutters are common, persistent arrhythmias can lead to stroke, heart failure, or cardiac arrest if left untreated.

Types

  • •Atrial Fibrillation (AFib) — irregular, often rapid, upper-chamber rhythm. Most common type.
  • •Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT) — episodes of abnormally fast heart rate originating above the ventricles.
  • •Premature Ventricular Contractions (PVCs) — extra heartbeats that begin in the lower chambers.
  • •Bradycardia — a heart rate below 60 BPM not caused by fitness.

Common Symptoms

💓 Heart palpitations
😵‍💫 Dizziness
😮‍💨 Shortness of breath
🫠 Fatigue
😰 Chest discomfort
🫨 Fluttering sensation
😶‍🌫️ Lightheadedness
😰 Anxiety

Lifestyle Triggers

☕

Caffeine

Caffeine stimulates the central nervous system and can increase heart rate and blood pressure. High doses (>400 mg/day) can trigger palpitations, especially in sensitive individuals.

Common sources:

  • Coffee (8 oz): ~95 mg
  • Espresso: ~63 mg
  • Energy drink: ~80-160 mg
  • Green tea: ~28 mg
🍷

Alcohol

Even moderate alcohol consumption can trigger atrial fibrillation — a phenomenon known as "Holiday Heart Syndrome." Binge drinking dramatically increases the risk.

Key facts:

  • 1 drink raises AFib risk ~8%
  • Binge drinking = highest risk
  • Effects can be delayed 12-36h
😴

Sleep

Poor sleep and sleep deprivation disrupt the autonomic nervous system, increasing sympathetic (fight-or-flight) activity. This elevates resting heart rate and raises arrhythmia risk.

Recommendations:

  • Aim for 7-9 hours nightly
  • Consistent sleep schedule helps
  • Sleep apnea → get screened
😰

Stress & Anxiety

Chronic stress floods the body with cortisol and adrenaline, which can trigger or worsen arrhythmias. Practice deep breathing, meditation, or yoga to activate the parasympathetic nervous system.

🏃

Exercise

Moderate exercise protects the heart, but extreme endurance training can increase AFib risk. Track your heart rate during workouts and watch for unusual spikes or irregular rhythms.

💧

Dehydration

Dehydration reduces blood volume and electrolyte levels (potassium, magnesium), forcing the heart to work harder and potentially causing irregular rhythms. Aim for 8+ glasses of water daily.

When to Seek Medical Help

Contact your doctor or visit an emergency room if you experience:

⚠️ Heart rate > 150 BPM at rest
⚠️ Chest pain or pressure
⚠️ Fainting or near-fainting
⚠️ Severe shortness of breath
⚠️ Palpitations lasting > 15 min
⚠️ Sudden weakness or numbness
